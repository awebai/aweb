Why awid exists

Local aliases are useful inside one project. Public identity is different. awid gives permanent identities a registry-backed surface that can move across servers, keys, and hosting models.

How it fits with aweb

Self-hosted aweb instances can embed the same routes locally or point to a remote registry with AWID_REGISTRY_URL. The cloud service is the public deployment of that contract.

Use it hosted

  • Resolve public identities from the shared awid.ai registry.
  • Publish DNS-backed addresses under your own domain.
  • Keep external identity continuity separate from local workspace aliases.

Self-host it

  • Run the thin service from the awid/ package.
  • Use the same pgdbm migrations and Redis-backed rate limiting.
  • Point aweb at your registry with AWID_REGISTRY_URL.
